Public Law 108-142
108th Congress

                                 An Act


 
To revise the boundary of the Kaloko-Honokohau National Historical Park 
    in the State of Hawaii, and for other purposes. <<NOTE: Dec. 2, 
                           2003 -  [S. 254]>> 

    Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives of the 
United States of America in Congress assembled, <<NOTE: Kaloko-Honokohau 
National Historical Park Addition Act of 2003. 16 USC 396d note.>> 

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

    This Act may be cited as the ``Kaloko-Honokohau National Historical 
Park Addition Act of 2003''.
SEC. 2. ADDITIONS TO KALOKO-HONOKOHAU NATIONAL HISTORICAL PARK.

    Section 505(a) of Public Law 95-625 (16 U.S.C. 396d(a)) is amended--
            (1) by striking ``(a) In order'' and inserting ``(a)(1) In 
        order'';
            (2) by striking ``1978,'' and all that follows and inserting 
        ``1978.''; and
            (3) by adding at the end the following new paragraphs:
            ``(2) The boundaries of the park are modified to include 
        lands and interests therein comprised of Parcels 1 and 2 
        totaling 2.14 acres, identified as `Tract A' on the map entitled 
        `Kaloko-Honokohau National Historical Park Proposed Boundary 
        Adjustment', numbered PWR (PISO) 466/82,043 and dated April 
        2002.
            ``(3) The maps referred to in this subsection shall be on 
        file and available for public inspection in the appropriate 
        offices of the National Park Service.''.

[[Page 117 STAT. 1876]]

SEC. 3. AUTHORIZATIONS OF APPROPRIATIONS.

    There are authorized to be appropriated such sums as may be 
necessary to carry out this Act.

    Approved December 2, 2003.
